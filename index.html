The Byzantine Empire, also known as the Eastern Roman Empire, emerged after the division of the Roman Empire in 395 AD. It became a successor to the ancient traditions, with its capital in Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul), founded by Emperor Constantine the Great.

The Golden Age of Byzantium fell during the reign of Emperor Justinian I (527-565 AD). During this period, great monuments were built, such as the Hagia Sophia, and the Code of Justinian was created, which had a significant impact on the development of the legal system of Europe.

The Byzantine Empire lasted for over a thousand years, but in 1453 it fell to the Ottoman Empire, when Turkish forces led by Sultan Mehmed II captured Constantinople. This year marks the end of the Middle Ages and the beginning of a new era in European history.
